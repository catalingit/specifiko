SM: Medtronic posted a 19% increase in its fiscal third quarter earnings today, to $831 million, or 75 cents per share, as compared to $698 million, or 62 cents per share, in the same quarter last year. The medical device maker said adjusted earnings totaled 77 cents per share, just ahead of analysts' estimates. Revenue rose 10% in the quarter, to $3.85 million. The company upped the low end of its fiscal 2010 forecast, saying it's now looking for earnings of $3.20 to $3.22 a share. Medtronic shares are trading lower by about 1.1% on Tuesday. In other pharma news, AstraZeneca hiked it 2010 earnings forecast after coming to an agreement with British regulators in a tax dispute. AstraZeneca agreed to pay a total 505 million pounds, or about $780 million, to settle the dispute. The company is now expecting earnings per share of $5.90 to $6.30, as compared to a prior forecast of $5.75 to $6.15 per share. AstraZeneca shares are trading lower by nearly 0.3% on Tuesday. And, Gilead Sciences said it's been cleared by the FDA to market its drug Cayston as an inhaled treatment to improve respiratory symptoms for cystic fibrosis patients with pseudomonas aeruginosa. The treatment was cleared in Australia last month, and Europe and Canada last fall. Gilead shares are trading lower by nearly 1% on Tuesday. For MarketNewsVideo.com, I'm Sayoko Murase.
